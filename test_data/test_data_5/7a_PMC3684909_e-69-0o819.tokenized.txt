supplementary crystallographic information comment the title phenyl benzoate ( scheme i , fig. 1 ) , which possesses an allyl and a methoxy substituent , was synthesized for an evaluation of its pharmaceutical properties as it is an ester derivative of eugenol .
the two benzene rings are approximately perpendicular [ dihedral angle 81.60 ( 5 ) degrees ] .
the twist is similar to that found in the unsubstituted compound , phenyl benzoate ( shibakami & sekiya , 1995 ) .
in the crystal , weak c - - h ... o hydrogen bond links molecules into the supramolecular chains extending along the a axis ( table 1 ) .
experimental 4 - allyl - 2 - methoxyphenol ( 1 mmol ) , 4 - methoxybenzoic acid ( 1 mmol ) , diethylazodicarboxylate ( 2 mmol ) and triphenylphosphine ( 2 mmol ) were heated in thf ( 10 ml ) for 2 h. the solid material extracted with dichloromethane .
the dichloromethane solution was eluted through a silica gel column by using an n - hexane - ethyl acetate ( 95 : 5 v / v ) solvent system .
slow evaporation of the solution yielded large colorless crystals .
